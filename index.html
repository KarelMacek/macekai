How it works

The orientation call is not a sales trap. It is a mutual check: what is really going on, whether coaching is the right tool, and whether it makes sense to work together.

01 — Book an orientation call

Free. 30 minutes. We talk about your situation. I will tell you honestly if I can help and what working together would look like.

02 — Decide freely on both sides

No pressure. If it makes sense for both of us, we define the goal and commit to the program.

03 — Commit seriously

Sessions via video call, 60–75 minutes. We go directly at the real question — not a template, not a process for its own sake.

04 — Work between sessions

Each session ends with a concrete action. The real transformation happens between sessions, not only during the conversation.

05 — Celebrate results and version 2.0

Real change that holds. A next chapter that actually fits your ability, your values, and the life you want to be living.

FAQ

How does my coaching actually work?

We meet regularly, usually for 3 or 12 sessions. I use brain-based coaching methods I trained in at the NeuroLeadership Institute. These methods work actively with principles of how the human brain makes sense of complex situations, decisions, pressure, and change.

My role is to navigate your thinking in a calm, structured, and efficient process toward the progress you want to achieve. Each session ends with a concrete action, because real change happens between sessions, not only during the conversation.

What is the ROI?

I cannot guarantee a specific result, because the real work is in your hands. The investment is partly the coaching fee, but your main part is following through on the actions you define, specify, and commit to between sessions.

In some cases, the financial return can substantially exceed the coaching fee, especially when income growth is an explicit focus. But I do not sell guaranteed outcomes. The deeper value is often clearer direction, better decisions, healthier performance, and a more integrated life.

That said, clients often value other outcomes even more: more meaningful work, better balance between delivery and learning, stronger professional direction, and healthier integration of professional and private life.

Why is your coaching unique?

I combine several worlds that are rarely brought together. I use brain-based coaching methods, but I also bring in ways of thinking from innovation and problem-solving, including TRIZ, Six Sigma, and design thinking. I draw on my PhD in optimal decision-making with probabilistically specified objectives — especially when helping clients clarify trade-offs, uncertainty, goals, and action.

There is also a deeper human layer. I wrote my MA thesis on anthropological foundations of personal development and have studied thinkers such as Simone Weil, Fernando Rielo, and Ludwig von Mises. In parallel, I collaborate with my wife on applied neuroscience and Montessori principles in family and community learning.

So the work is not only "career coaching" or "leadership coaching." It is structured thinking, human development, decision-making, and real-life action brought into one process.

Why is it not just advice-giving?

Advice-giving rarely works in complex life and leadership situations. You do not need someone to win your battle for you. You need to win your own victory.

My role is not to fight instead of you, but to offer a spotlight, a calm mind, and a structured process. Most people today do not lack information. They need to harmonize what they already know, see the situation more clearly, and turn it into a successful journey toward their goals.

That does not mean advice has no value. In some cases it can be useful, and I am open to it. But then we are operating under a different contract. I see advice-giving as an exception, not the core of the work.

My top priority is coaching, because I believe in people, their aspirations, and their inner resources.

So if it is not about advice, why focus on AI team leaders?

Because I know this world from the inside. These are not abstract "target clients" for me. They are people whose pressures, language, ambitions, overload, and professional dilemmas I understand from lived experience.

I have gone through many of the pains myself. That helps me sense more precisely what is being said, what is not being said, and where the real tension may be. Compared with a random coach, I can often recognize the technical, organizational, and psychological patterns faster.

Clients also value my technical analogies and metaphors. When useful, I explain how the mind works, why certain patterns appear under pressure, and how we can use these principles for their own journey.

The focus on AI team leaders is not about giving them technical advice. It is about creating a coaching space where their world is deeply understood.
